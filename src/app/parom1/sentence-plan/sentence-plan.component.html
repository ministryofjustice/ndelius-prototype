Prison sentence plan and response

  • Current and future sentence plan objectives and other agencies involved.
  • Evidence of the prisoner’s engagement in the process.
  • Evidence of the prisoner understanding the purpose of interventions and taking responsibility for their agreed objectives.
  • Objectives that meet all risk, need and responsivity considerations.
  • Evidence of the 7 priority areas linked to women and their ability to stay safe and reduce the likelihood of further offending. They are substance misuse, mental health, emotion management, pro-social identity, control of daily life, family contact and resettlement and building social capital.
  • An objective, if relevant, to address specific child, adult or victim safeguarding - you should record if this cannot be completed at the current establishment or before release.
  • Consideration how an OPD formulation influences their sentence plan.
  • Encouragement to set positive goals to reduce the risk of further harmful behaviour if the prisoner is in denial. For example, working through possible domestic abuse and healthy relationship situations.
  • Planned activity responsive to the prisoner’s age, gender, race and cultural background.
  • Planned activity responsive to the prisoner’s psychosocial maturity and diversity considerations such as learning difficulties or disabilities.
  • Analysis of the prisoner’s response to their progress.
  • Analysis of the work the prisoner is required to complete if they are ineligible (pre-tariff or in hospital) for release, or if they are seeking a move to open conditions.
