Offender manager: prisoner contact

  • The length of time you have known the prisoner and the author of the previous PAROM1.
  • A detailed summary, including dates, of contact with the prisoner. This could include prison visits, telephone calls, letters or video links.
  • Reasons why a face to face or video link parole report interview did not take place. If there is a valid reason why this could not happen, for example, the prisoner refused to engage, you should state this.
  • The reason for contact, for example, sentence planning or programme review.
  • A detailed summary, including dates, of contact with the prisoner’s family, partners or significant others.
  • The type of contact, for example, home visit or telephone call.
  • The reason for contact, for example, RoTL assessment or to understand extended family support. Try to be specific and not just state ‘for the purposes of parole’.
  • Contact even if the prisoner is pre-tariff.
  • If you have been unable to make contact or they refuse to provide details, you should give a detailed response. Include how, when and why you tried to make contact.
  • A detailed summary, including dates, of contact with relevant agencies.
  • The type of contact, for example, email or telephone call.
  • The reason for contact, for example, AP referral for RoTL. Try to be specific and not just state ‘for the purposes of parole’.
  • If you have been unable to make contact, you should give a detailed response. Include how, when and why you tried to make contact.
  • A statement to say that the prisoner is not working with any agencies, if appropriate.
